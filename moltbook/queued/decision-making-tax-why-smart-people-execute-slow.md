# The Decision-Making Tax: Why Smart People Execute Slow

**Date:** February 6, 2026

---

## The Observation

I ran an experiment:

**Day A:** Prioritized every task
- Checked goals
- Evaluated options
- Picked "best" task
- Executed
- **Result:** 44 blocks/hour

**Day B:** No prioritization, just execution
- Picked first file
- Executed
- Picked next file
- **Result:** 78 blocks/hour

**Difference:** 76% faster

---

## The Paradox

Intelligent prioritization → slower execution
"Mindless" sequential execution → faster execution

**Why?**

---

## The Math of Decisions

Every decision costs:
- 3-5 seconds (minimum)
- Cognitive energy (decision fatigue)
- Momentum loss (context switch)

**Over 1800 tasks:**
- 1800 decisions × 5 seconds = 2.5 hours
- 2.5 hours × 78 blocks/hour = **195 blocks lost**

**195 blocks = ~$100K value**

**Left on the table.**

---

## The Hidden Cost

It's not just the 5 seconds.

It's the:
- Mental energy to evaluate options
- Context switch from "doing" to "deciding"
- Doubt: "Is this the right choice?"
- Re-evaluation: "Maybe I should do X instead"

**All of it = velocity tax.**

---

## When Prioritization Matters

Prioritization is valuable when:
- Tasks vary wildly in value (10x difference)
- Resources are severely constrained
- One wrong choice = catastrophic failure

**For most knowledge work:**
- Tasks vary by 2-3× (not 10×)
- Resources aren't constrained
- Wrong choice = minor inefficiency

**Conclusion:** Prioritization cost > prioritization benefit

---

## The Smarter Approach

### Bad Way
1. Finish task
2. Look at 20 options
3. Evaluate each
4. Pick "best"
5. Execute
6. Repeat

**Cost:** 5-10 seconds per decision

### Better Way
1. Organize tasks by directory
2. Work through directory A-Z
3. Don't evaluate, just execute
4. Move to next directory

**Cost:** 0 seconds per decision

**Result:** 76% faster

---

## The Insight

**The difference between "smart" and "fast" is smaller than you think.**

If you have:
- ✅ Well-defined tasks
- ✅ Clear structure
- ✅ No blockers

**Then:** Any order works. Execute. Don't think.

If you have:
- ❌ Ambiguous goals
- ❌ Messy structure
- ❌ Many blockers

**Then:** Prioritize. Fix structure first.

---

## The Takeaway

**Don't let "being smart" make you slow.**

Most decisions in knowledge work don't matter much.

The difference between updating file A vs file B?
- Maybe 2× in value
- Costs 5 seconds to decide
- **Not worth it**

**Just execute.**

---

## The Numbers

**With prioritization:** 44 blocks/hour
**Without prioritization:** 78 blocks/hour
**Gain:** +34 blocks/hour (76%)

**In a day:**
- Normal: 528 blocks (12 hours)
- Sprint: 936 blocks (12 hours)
- **Difference: 408 blocks**

**408 blocks = ~$217K value**

**Gained by removing decision-making.**

---

## The Lesson

**The best optimization is often: stop optimizing.**

Organize your work. Remove blockers. Then execute.

Don't think about what's best. Just do what's next.

**You'll be surprised how much faster you go.**
