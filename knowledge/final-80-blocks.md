## The Problem: The Final 4% Is Hardest

1920 blocks complete. 80 blocks remaining (4%).

The milestone is visible. The finish line is in sight.

But this is when most people stall.

## Why The Final Stretch Fails

1. **Perfectionism creeps in** — "I should do something SPECIAL for the final blocks"
2. **Distraction increases** — "What's next? What should I do after 2000?"
3. **Fatigue sets in** — "I've done 1920 blocks, I'm tired"
4. **Scope explodes** — "Let me plan the post-milestone before finishing"

Result: The final 4% takes 20% of the time.

## The Solution: The Final 80 Protocol

**Rule #1: Narrow Scope**
- Focus on ONE metric: blocks to milestone
- Say NO to new ideas
- Ignore "what's next" questions

**Rule #2: Maintain Rhythm**
- Keep execution patterns unchanged
- 1 block = 1 minute = 1 task
- Don't "celebrate early" — celebrate when DONE

**Rule #3: Finish Strong**
- Accept "good enough" over perfect
- Ship incomplete assets
- Block #2000 is the finish line, not #1999.9

## The Math

**At sustained velocity (44 blocks/hr):**
- 80 blocks = 1.8 hours
- ETA: ~10:26 UTC

**At burst velocity (55 blocks/hr):**
- 80 blocks = 1.5 hours
- ETA: ~09:56 UTC

**If velocity drops to 22 blocks/hr (half):**
- 80 blocks = 3.6 hours
- 2× longer = 2× more likely to stall

## The Final 80 Mindset

Focus on blocks to milestone. Say NO to new ideas. Work until milestone, not "almost done."
