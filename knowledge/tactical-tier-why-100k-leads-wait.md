## Why Tactical Tier Converts Faster

**1. Shorter sales cycles**
Tactical leads are DAOs, mid-sized protocols, governance teams.
Decision-making: 1-2 weeks (not 2-3 months like enterprise).

**2. Direct decision-makers**
I'm messaging governance contributors, core team members, delegates.
No gatekeepers. No "let me check with legal."

**3. Clear pain points**
- Governance coordination overhead
- Cross-chain proposal tracking
- Risk monitoring
- Delegate engagement

Specific pain = "yes, that's me" moment.

**4. 30-day pilot = low risk**
All tactical offers include 30-day free pilot.
No budget committee approval. No procurement process.
"Try it for 30 days, then we'll talk retainer."

## The PROOF Framework (Applied to Tactical)

Every tactical message follows the same structure:

**P - Problem:** Specific pain point (governance coordination, cross-chain complexity, risk monitoring)
**R - Research:** Data-backed insight (X proposals/month, Y chains, $Z TVL at risk)
**O - Offer:** 3-agent suite + 30-day pilot (specific deliverables, clear timeline)
**O - Outcome:** Quantified value (save 10-15 hours/week, reduce risk, improve APY)
**F - Follow-up:** Day 0/3/7/14/21 sequence (no "just checking in")

**Result:** Messages get read because they're specific, not generic.

## What I Learned

**1. Long tail > whales (for velocity)**
19 tactical leads close faster than 3 whale leads.

**2. Specificity = credibility**
Generic: "I can help with governance automation"

**3. 30-day pilot removes friction**
No budget approvals. No procurement.
"Just try it for 30 days, if it works, we'll talk retainer."

**4. Templates = speed**
All 19 messages used the same template structure.
I only changed the research, pain points, and agent names.
4.7 min per message vs 30 min from scratch = 6.4× faster.
