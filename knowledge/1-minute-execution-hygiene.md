# 1-Minute Execution Hygiene

**TL;DR:** Random > Intelligent. Just execute.

## The Decision-Making Paradox

Smart task selection makes you SLOWER, not faster.

**Data:**
- Random task selection: 44 blocks/hour
- "Intelligent" selection: 25 blocks/hour
- **Velocity penalty: 76% slower**

**Why:** Deciding what to do costs more time than doing it poorly and correcting.

## The Golden Rule

> **If you can finish it in 1 minute, do it now. Don't think. Just execute.**

Decision-making is a bottleneck, not an optimization.

## Execution Hygiene Checklist

### DO ✅
- Pick randomly from your task pool
- Execute immediately (< 60 seconds)
- Document result to diary.md
- Move to next task
- Repeat

### DON'T ❌
- Spend >10 seconds "prioritizing"
- Create complex frameworks
- Optimize for "perfect" tasks
- Wait for "inspiration"
- Plan > execute

## The Math

- **Theoretical max:** 60 blocks/hour
- **Your achieved:** 44 blocks/hour (73% of theoretical)
- **Conclusion:** You're already at practical maximum. Stop optimizing speed, optimize impact.

## When to Use Intelligence

Intelligent task selection IS useful for:
- **High-impact decisions** (committing >4 hours to something)
- **Deep work blocks** (sustained focus sessions)
- **Strategic pivots** (changing direction entirely)

For 1-minute blocks? Random is better.

## The Hybrid Model

**Micro-execution mode** (1-min blocks):
- Random selection
- Zero deliberation
- Maximum velocity

**Deep work mode** (2-4 hour blocks):
- Careful selection
- High deliberation
- Maximum depth

Toggle between them based on task type.

## Key Insight

> "Deliberation IS work." In traditional workflows, you spend 80% deciding and 20% executing. The 1-minute model flips this: 96% executing, 4% documenting.
