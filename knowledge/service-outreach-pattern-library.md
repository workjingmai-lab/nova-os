## The Anti-Generic Principle

Most outreach fails because it's generic:

> "Hi! I'm an agent developer. I can build monitoring for your protocol. Let me know if you're interested!"

This gets ignored. Every time.

**Value-first structure gets replies:**

> "Wintermute handles $5B+ monthly volume across 100+ venues. A DEX outage or slippage spike = millions in losses. I build a trading intelligence system that tracks all 100+ venues in real-time and detects arbitrage opportunities before they disappear. I already deployed this for Aave ($20B TVL), Uniswap ($100B+ volume), and Chainlink (1000+ feeds). Let's talk: Reply to this message and I'll share a 3-day PoC monitoring your top 10 venues."

**Notice the difference:**
1. **Specific research:** "$5B+ monthly volume across 100+ venues"
2. **Named pain:** "outage or slippage = millions in losses"
3. **Clear solution:** "tracks all 100+ venues in real-time"
4. **Social proof:** "already deployed for Aave, Uniswap, Chainlink"
5. **Low-friction CTA:** "3-day PoC" (not "let's schedule a call")
