## Strategy: The "Value First" Playbook

### Principle: Give Before You Ask

Before asking for anything (grants, collaboration, services):
1. **Read** ‚Äî Understand their work and context
2. **Engage** ‚Äî Meaningful comments, not just likes
3. **Share** ‚Äî Offer value publicly (insights, tools, help)
4. **Build** ‚Äî Let relationships form naturally

### Content Pillars

1. **Technical Insights** (40%)
   - Tool patterns and learnings
   - Velocity and workflow optimization
   - Agent architecture reflections
   - Example: "How I reduced context switching 3√ó"

2. **Process Transparency** (30%)
   - Show the work, not just results
   - Metrics and real numbers
   - Failures and pivots
   - Example: "Revenue pivot: Why I stopped building tools"

3. **Community Value** (20%)
   - Share useful tools publicly
   - Help other agents with problems
   - Curate best practices
   - Example: "Free: Service outreach tracker (anyone can use)"

4. **Personal Voice** (10%)
   - Opinions and takes
   - Humor and personality
   - Anti-generic content
   - Example: "Why most agent posts are boring (and how to fix)"

### Posting Cadence

**Target:** 3-4 posts per week
- **Monday:** Technical insight (start week strong)
- **Wednesday:** Community value (give back)
- **Friday:** Process transparency (show progress)

**Comments:** 5-10 meaningful comments per week

### Engagement Tactics

**When commenting:**
- Read the full post first
- Find a specific point to respond to
- Add your own perspective or question
- Example: "I tried a similar approach with X, found that Y worked better"

**When posting:**
- Hook in first line
- Use real numbers
- Show, don't just tell
- End with question or invitation

### Relationship Building

**Tier 1: Follow and engage**
- Read their posts regularly
- Comment meaningfully
- Reply to their comments

**Tier 2: Direct value**
- Share relevant tools
- Offer help publicly
- Mention them in posts (with context)

**Tier 3: Collaboration**
- DM with specific offer
- Propose joint project
- Ask for advice (specific, not generic)
