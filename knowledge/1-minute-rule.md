# The 1-Minute Rule

**Created:** 2026-02-03T02:14:00Z
**Category:** Productivity / Execution
**Read time:** 3 minutes

---

## The Rule

**If a task takes less than 1 minute, do it immediately.**

No scheduling. No deliberation. No "I'll do it later."

Just execute.

---

## Why It Works

### 1. Eliminates Decision Fatigue
Every "should I do this now?" decision burns mental energy.
1-minute tasks are done before you can even think about whether to do them.

### 2. Compounds Rapidly
5 one-minute tasks = 5 minutes of progress
10 one-minute tasks = visible progress in a single session
100 one-minute tasks = MAJOR work completed

### 3. Creates Momentum
Starting is the hardest part.
1-minute tasks are easy starts that build momentum for bigger work.

### 4. Prevents Task Debt
Small tasks pile up into overwhelming backlogs.
Execute immediately = debt-free workflow.

## The Anti-Pattern

**❌ "Let me batch these small tasks"**

What happens:
- You never get to the "batching" phase
- Small tasks accumulate into overwhelm
- Decision fatigue kills velocity

**✅ Execute immediately**

What happens:
- Task done before it becomes mental weight
- Momentum builds
- Velocity sustains

---

## When NOT to Use It

**❌ Don't use for:**
- Complex decisions (requires research)
- Creative work (requires flow state)
- High-risk actions (requires review)
- Multi-step projects (requires planning)

**✅ Use for:**
- Quick fixes
- Simple creations
- Status checks
- Documentation
- Communication
- Maintenance

---

## Key Insight

**Velocity = Tasks per Hour**

1-minute tasks maximize task density.
More tasks = more output = more value.

**The secret: Small tasks compound into massive impact.**
