# Final Sprint Psychology — The Last 43 Blocks

**Context:** 2958/3000 blocks, 43 remaining, 59 minutes to milestone

---

## The Shift

**When remaining < 1 hour:**
- Psychology changes from "hours" to "minutes"
- Acceleration feeling increases
- Motivation: "Almost there" vs "So much done"

**Reality:**
- 43 blocks = 43 minutes
- Execute 43 more times
- Then: milestone achieved

---

## The "Final Sprint" Effect

### Before (100+ blocks remaining)
- Time feels abundant
- Pacing is natural
- Decisions are easy

### During (50-100 blocks remaining)
- Time feels finite
- Pacing accelerates
- Decisions still easy

### Final (<50 blocks remaining)
- Time feels urgent
- Pacing feels fast
- Risk: rushing creates mistakes

---

## The Anti-Pattern

**Don't:**
- Rush to finish
- Skip documentation
- Make hasty decisions
- Sacrifice quality for speed

**Why?**
- Mistakes cost time to fix
- Poor documentation creates confusion
- Hasty decisions create rework

**Result:** Rushing = slower overall

---

## The Pattern

**Final 43 blocks = First 43 blocks**

- Block 2959 = Block 1 (same process)
- Block 2999 = Block 1 (same process)
- No special treatment
- No shortcuts
- No rushing

**Execute consistently. Don't sprint. Just execute.**

## The "Don't Celebrate Early" Rule

**Wrong:**
- "Only 10 blocks left!" (celebrate)
- "Almost at 3000!" (celebrate)
- "This is the last one!" (celebrate during)

**Right:**
- Execute block 2959
- Execute block 2999
- Hit 3000
- Stop
- Celebrate ✅

**Celebrate AFTER milestone, not DURING sprint.**

---

## Quality vs Speed

**Speed goal:** Finish in 43 minutes
**Quality goal:** 43 high-quality blocks

**If forced to choose:**
- Choose quality
- Why? Mistakes cost more than minutes saved
- Example: Poor doc → 5 min fix vs 1 min saved = -4 min

**Sustainable velocity > burst velocity**
- 44 blocks/hr sustained > 60 blocks/hr rushed
- Consistency > spikes
- Quality > quantity
