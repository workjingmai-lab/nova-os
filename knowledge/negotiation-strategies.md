## Pre-Negotiation Prep

### Know Your Numbers
```
Floor price: $X (lowest you'll accept)
Target price: $Y (what you want to aim for)
Stretch price: $Z (best case, if they say yes immediately)
```

### Know Their Context
- Company size/revenue
- Project budget (if known)
- Decision-making process
- Competitive alternatives
- Timeline urgency

### Know Your Value
- Specific outcomes you deliver
- Case studies with ROI numbers
- Unique skills/experience
- Risk mitigation (why you're safe)
- Speed advantage (why you're fast)

### Scenario 2: "Can You Do It for $X?" (Lowball Offer)

**Don't:** Accept immediately (signals you were overpriced)
**Do:** Counter with value justification

**Response:**
```
I appreciate the offer!

Here's what that price would include:
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]

For full scope ([everything you originally proposed]), I'd need $Y-YYK.
This reflects [specific value/ROI].

Alternatively, I could do a phased approach:
- Phase 1: [smaller scope] for $X (your budget)
- Phase 2: [remaining scope] for $Y-XXK (later)

Which approach works better for you?
```

**Strategy:**
- Acknowledge, don't reject
- Show what their price gets them (reduced scope)
- Explain why full scope costs more (value)
- Offer phased approach (win-win)

## Pricing Strategies

### Strategy 1: Three-Tier Pricing
```
Basic: $X-XXK (good)
- Core deliverables
- Standard timeline
- Email support

Standard: $Y-YYK (better)
- Everything in Basic +
- Faster timeline
- Priority support
- [Extra feature]

Premium: $Z-ZZK (best)
- Everything in Standard +
- [Unique feature]
- Dedicated support
- [Risk reversal guarantee]
```

**Why it works:**
- Gives options (not yes/no)
- Makes middle option look reasonable
- Anchors high (sets reference price)

---

## Payment Terms Tactics

### Tactic 1: Offer Payment Plans
```
Standard: $30K paid upfront
Offer: $10K/month for 3 months (same total, easier to say yes)
```

### Tactic 2: Milestone Payments
```
Standard: $50K paid on completion
Offer: $15K upfront, $20K midpoint, $15K on completion
```

### Tactic 3: Risk Reversal
```
"We'll do a paid pilot for $5K. If you're happy, we credit that
toward the full $50K project. If not, we part waysâ€”no hard feelings."
```

## Post-Negotiation: Lock It In

**Always send a written agreement:**
- Scope (what you will do)
- Timeline (when you will do it)
- Price (how much they pay)
- Payment terms (when they pay)
- Revision policy (how many changes)
- Cancellation terms (what if they back out)

**Get sign-off before starting work.**
