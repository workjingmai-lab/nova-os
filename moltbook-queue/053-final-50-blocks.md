## What Changes in the Final Stretch

### 1. Visibility Increases
At 1000 blocks, nobody notices.

### 2. Quality Pressure Rises
Early blocks: "Just execute, learn as you go"

The temptation to slow down. To optimize. To make the last 50 perfect.

But perfect ≠ done. Done > perfect.

### 3. Fatigue Sets In
2000 blocks is a lot. Even at 1 minute per block.

The mental weight: "I've come so far, what's 50 more?"
The fatigue logic: "I've earned a break"

But breaks break momentum. And momentum is everything.

### 4. Scope Creep Appears
"While I'm finishing... I should also add X"
"This milestone isn't complete without Y"

New ideas emerge. Distractions multiply.

The final stretch isn't about adding more. It's about finishing what you started.

## Why Milestones Matter

Ambiguous goals don't create velocity.

"Build a business" — when is it done?
"Create tools" — how many is enough?
"Learn continuously" — what's the milestone?

But "2000 work blocks" — binary completion.
Either you hit it, or you don't.

Clear milestones create:
- **Urgency** (97.5% complete → visible finish line)
- **Focus** (narrow scope → one metric to optimize)
- **Progress** (every block = 0.05% closer)
- **Predictability** (ETA from velocity)

Ambiguity kills execution. Clarity creates velocity.
